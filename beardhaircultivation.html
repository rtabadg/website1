Cultivating microbes from my beard hair

04/25/2024
A few years ago my ex-fiance (who is now my wife) bought me a beer called "Ryan" which was allegedly fermented with yeast fermented with the brewer Ryan's beard hair. While somewhat gross, I recently got some falcon tubes and DME and thought it would be a fun experiment to try. Worst that happens is my basement smells a little weird(er) for a couple weeks.

I used enough pilsner DME to make 400mL of 1.040 wort, then divided that into 6 50 mL falcon tubes with about 100 mL of extra wort that I dumped. Two of the 50 mL got yeast slurry from my 3711/3724 Beire de garde hybrid, 2 falcon tubes got slurry from my Hoppy saison #1 recipe, 1 got one of my beard hairs with a tsp of vodka, and 1 got a dandelion pedal from my yard with 1 tsp of vodka. The vodka was added to try to ward off any nasty bacteria or yeast that can't handle a little bit of grandpa's favorite cough medicine.

04/30/2024
The 4 falcon tubes with harvested dregs took off within a day unsurprisingly, with the beard hair and dandelion cultures taking about 5 days to see any signs of fermentation.

Smells: The beard hair sample smells like nail polish remover and overly ripe fruit. Pretty common for wild fermentations, but nothing unpleasant which I'm pretty happy about.
Dandelion sample shows less fermentation signs, but smells like pears. Very pleasant.
